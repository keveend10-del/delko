# Discovery Call Guide

## Sales Process

1. Research the business (website, GBP, reviews, competitors, social, AI visibility)
2. Identify the main gaps
3. Start a conversation — don't hard pitch
4. Offer a short free audit with 3–5 ideas
5. Run discovery call
6. Present a simple proposal
7. Close around ROI: one extra job can pay for the service
8. Onboard and deliver fast
9. Capture proof / case study

---

## Discovery Call Questions

Ask these in order. Listen more than you talk.

**Finding you:**
- How are most customers finding you right now?
- What type of customer or job do you want more of?

**Business:**
- What is your average job worth?
- What has worked before in marketing?
- What felt like a waste of money?

**Current gaps:**
- How do you currently follow up with leads?
- How many leads do you think you miss each month?

**Decision:**
- What would make marketing feel worth it to you?
- What would make you hesitant to move forward?

---

## Objection Notes

**"I tried SEO before and it didn't work."**
What they usually mean: they paid for content or link-building that didn't connect to calls. What we do is different — GBP, reviews, local pages, and follow-up. Ask what specifically didn't work and what their expectation was.

**"I don't have the budget."**
Ask: what's an average job worth? Then show the math. One extra job per month often covers Starter Presence.

**"I need to think about it."**
Ask: what's the main thing you'd want to be sure about before saying yes? Then address that directly.

**"I'm not locked into a contract, right?"**
Confirm: month-to-month. No cancellation fees. We stay because the work works.

---

## Closing Framework

1. Summarize what you heard: "It sounds like your main issue is X."
2. Connect to the outcome: "If we got that fixed, you'd see more [calls / quote requests / bookings]."
3. Show the ROI math: "Your average job is $Y. If we get you just one more job per month..."
4. Recommend a specific plan: "Given what you've told me, I'd start with Local Growth System."
5. Ask for the business: "Does that feel like the right first step?"
